Are the impoverished at risk?

U.S. Census Bureau and Center for Disease Control publicly available data were visualized and analyzed to explore potential associations between health risks and income.

Poverty and the Uninsured:

Individuals who have a lower income are more likely to be uninsured. However, we should consider other factors such as age that may skew this simplified statement (e.g not all uninsured have low-income, but may simply be young and have a 'super(wo)man complex'.

Poverty and Smoking:

Individuals living below the poverty level have higher rates of cigarette smoking than the general population. Considering other psycho-social elements such as stress and depressive symptoms may contirbute to the higher level of smokers.

Poverty and Obesity:

It is suggested that higher rates of obesity and being over weight occur in impoverished areas. This could be due to the lack of nutritious or fresh foods or access to them; other foods that are considered less healthy are more readily available and, more importantly, cheaper.

This just scrapes the surface, but it is clear that poverty is associated with numerous negative health outcomes, including but not limited to the ones mentioned above.
